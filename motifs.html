Key Javanese Motifs & Their Meaning


Parang

Diagonal S-shaped pattern inspired by ocean waves. Symbolises continuity, resilience, and spiritual struggle. Historically associated with royalty.

Kawung (Ceplok)

Rounded or cross-shaped repeated motif. Often read as purity and balance. Frequently used in court batik.

Sido Mukti / Sidoluhur

Elaborate floral motif meaning prosperity and dignity, commonly used for weddings and rites of passage.

Truntum

Flower-like motif meaning "to grow love", often used for wedding garments and blessings.

Mega Mendung

Cloud-like band patterns (originating from coastal styles). Symbolizes calm, blessing, and spiritual protection.

Sekar Jagad

Floral patterns symbolizing the universe

Buketan

Bouquet-like floral arrangements.

Garuda

Stylized mythical bird (Garuda).
